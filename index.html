Xifaxan Updates▼

Q1 2026 Revenue Strong — 21% Growth

Salix reported Q1 2026 revenues of $639M (+$97M / +18% vs. Q1 2025), with Xifaxan specifically posting 21% revenue growth. The core franchise remains healthy despite pipeline setbacks.

Source: Bausch Health Q1 2026 Form 8-K (SEC) ↗

RED-C Phase 3 Program Failed (January 2026)

Both RED-C-3131 and RED-C-3132 — the Phase 3 trials evaluating amorphous-rifaximin SSD for primary prevention of HE in liver cirrhosis — missed their primary endpoints. Enrolled 1,000+ patients across 398 sites in 17 countries. The drug was safe and well-tolerated, but did not significantly delay first HE onset vs. placebo. Bausch is reviewing the dataset for "potential new development opportunities." This was a meaningful pipeline loss: no approved preventative therapy for HE currently exists.

Source: Clinical Trials Arena ↗ | Clival ↗

Competitive Intelligence▼

HE (Hepatic Encephalopathy)

2026 ACG Clinical Guideline — Rifaximin Reinforced

The American College of Gastroenterology published its updated HE guideline in March 2026. Key rifaximin positions:

  • TIPS prophylaxis: Strong recommendation — initiate rifaximin 14 days pre-TIPS, continue ≥6 months
  • Recurrent OHE prevention: Conditional recommendation for rifaximin in outpatient setting
  • Acute OHE: Guideline suggests adding rifaximin to lactulose (adjunct, not replacement)
  • Lactulose + rifaximin remains the standard combination; zinc supplementation added for persistent symptoms with low serum zinc

This guideline update is favorable for Xifaxan's label indications and should support formulary positioning.

Source: ACG Clinical Guideline — American Journal of Gastroenterology ↗ | Guideline Central Summary ↗

Pipeline HE Therapies — Early Stage

Emerging therapies in the HE pipeline include VE303 (Vedanta Biosciences), YAQ007 (Yaqrit), IV albumin 25% (Grifols), EBX-102 (EnteroBiotix), and GR3027 (Umecrine). None are near approval; market growth projected at modest 1.2% CAGR through 2036.

Source: GlobeNewswire / DelveInsight ↗

MASTERPIECE Trial (Microbiota Transplant)

Strategic Signals▼

1. RED-C Failure Creates HE Prevention Vacuum — Watch Competitors

No therapy is approved for primary HE prevention in cirrhosis. The RED-C failure doesn't open this space to Xifaxan (wrong indication), but it does signal the difficulty of proving prevention. Pipeline players (VE303, GR3027, microbiota approaches) may accelerate investment into this unmet need. Worth tracking whether any of these pivot toward IND/Phase 2 urgency.

2. ACG Guideline Favorable — But Conditional Language Limits Formulary Leverage

The new ACG guideline reinforces rifaximin's role in HE management, but the conditional recommendation language (vs. a strong recommendation) for outpatient recurrent OHE could limit payer leverage. Salix's market access team should be engaging payers on this language gap now, especially with generics pending.
