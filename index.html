Xifaxan Updates▼

Q1 2026 Revenue Beat. Salix segment revenues hit $639M in Q1 2026, up from $542M in Q1 2025 (+18%). Xifaxan itself grew 21% in the quarter — a strong commercial performance despite pipeline setbacks. Source: Bausch Health Q1 2026 8-K ↗

RED-C Phase III Failure (January 23, 2026). Bausch Health's rifaximin SSD (amorphous solid soluble dispersion), a next-generation reformulation positioned as Xifaxan's successor in HE primary prevention, failed both Phase III trials. The RED-C program enrolled 1,000+ patients across 398 sites in 17 countries — neither trial met the primary endpoint of delaying first HE episode in cirrhosis patients with no prior HE history. Bausch recorded a $1.426B goodwill impairment in Q1 2026 as a result. The company is reviewing the full dataset for potential new development paths but has no clear Plan B for HE expansion. Sources: Clinical Trials Arena ↗ · FiercePharma ↗ · Citeline/Scrip ↗

Pipeline Pivot. Post-RED-C, Bausch Health is advancing larsucosterol for alcohol-associated hepatitis as a key pipeline asset. No new HE indication expansion announced. Source: Bausch Health Q1 2026 8-K ↗

Supply & Access Issues. Xifaxan is not on the FDA official drug shortage list, but patient-reported access difficulties persist due to high cost ($1,400–$1,800 per IBS-D course; $60–$100 per TD course), limited generic competition, and insurance restrictions. Sources: Medfinder ↗ · MedicalNewsToday ↗

Competitive Intelligence▼

HE Standard of Care Reinforced. The American College of Gastroenterology published updated HE guidelines in March 2026. Key recommendation: for patients with overt HE on lactulose experiencing recurrent episodes, rifaximin is recommended as add-on therapy. Zinc supplementation is added for persistent symptoms despite lactulose + rifaximin. The guidelines maintain Xifaxan's entrenched position in the recurrent HE maintenance protocol. Source: Guideline Central – March 2026 ACG HE Guideline ↗

HE Pipeline Threat Assessment. Several therapies are in active development that could eventually compete with or complement rifaximin in HE:

  • VE303 (Vedanta Biosciences) — microbiome-based; Phase 2 for HE treatment (~100 patients in trials)
  • GR3027 (Umecrine Cognition) — GABA-A neurosteroid antagonist; anticipated US filing ~2027
  • EBX-102 (EnteroBiotix) — microbiome-based
  • YAQ007 (Yaqrit Discovery) — mechanism targets hyperammonemia
  • 25% IV Albumin (Grifols) — adjunctive HE management
  • MASTERPIECE trial — microbiota transplant capsules vs. HE recurrence in veterans with cirrhosis, ongoing

Source: DelveInsight HE Market Report, May 2026 ↗ · Guideline Central – May 2026 HE Trial Preview ↗

IBS-D Competitive Landscape. Xifaxan remains the dominant antibiotic/microbiome-targeting IBS-D therapy with $800M+ in annual revenues historically.

  • Viberzi (eluxadoline) — limited growth due to prescribing restrictions (pancreatitis risk); AbbVie exploring safety improvements as of February 2026
  • Linzess (linaclotide) — IBS-C focused; ~$864.5M US net sales in 2025; generic linaclotide now widely available, adding pricing pressure in adjacent IBS-C space
  • Ibsrela (tenapanor) — IBS-C focused; $274.2M in 2025 (+73% YoY); supply access issues reported despite no FDA shortage listing
  • Lotronex (alosetron) — niche use in severe IBS-D in women; no material competitive movement

Source: Xtalks – IBS Awareness Month 2026 ↗

Strategic Signals▼

Xifaxan revenue is growing despite pipeline failure. The 21% Q1 2026 revenue growth is notable — commercial execution at Salix is strong even as the long-term succession plan collapsed. This makes defending the patent wall through 2029 the single most important near-term priority for Bausch/Salix.

RED-C failure creates a strategic gap in primary HE prevention. No approved therapy exists for primary prevention in cirrhosis patients without prior HE episodes. Pipeline entrants (GR3027, VE303) are targeting exactly this unmet need. Xifaxan is not positioned here — its label covers recurrent HE, not primary prevention.

ACG guidelines are a tailwind. The March 2026 ACG guideline update explicitly recommends rifaximin add-on for recurrent HE on lactulose. This reinforces prescriber behavior and creates headroom for continued formulary positioning.

HE market growth modest but durable. DelveInsight projects the HE market at 1.2% CAGR through 2036 — not explosive, but stable. Rifaximin's position as the established standard of care makes it hard to displace absent a major clinical disruption.

IBS-D: no immediate competitive disruption. The IBS-D pipeline remains sparse. High-growth IBS competitors (Ibsrela, Linzess) are IBS-C focused and don't directly threaten Xifaxan's IBS-D positioning. The real threat is generic entry ~2029, not brand competition.
