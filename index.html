Xifaxan Updates▼

RED-C Phase 3 Trial Failure (Jan 2026) — Major Story

Bausch Health's RED-C program — two global Phase 3 trials testing a new amorphous-rifaximin solid soluble dispersion (SSD) formulation for primary prevention of hepatic encephalopathy in cirrhosis patients — failed to meet the primary endpoint. Both trials were safe and well-tolerated, but neither demonstrated statistically significant delay of first HE episode vs. placebo. The trials enrolled 1,000+ patients across 398 sites in 17 countries. Bausch Health stock dropped 12.4% on the announcement. This removes the most significant growth lever Bausch had for Xifaxan beyond current exclusivity.

  • Source: Bausch Health 8-K / Press Release ↗ | Clinical Trials Arena ↗ | Pharmaphorum ↗

Salix Q1 2026 Financials — Strong Despite Setback

Salix segment revenues hit $639M in Q1 2026 vs. $542M Q1 2025 (+18%). Xifaxan specifically grew 21% year-over-year. Core brand remains commercially healthy, but analysts note the RED-C failure means revenue growth post-patent expiry becomes a serious strategic gap.

  • Source: Bausch Health Q1 2026 8-K ↗

Supply Availability

Xifaxan is not on the FDA official drug shortage list, but persistent pharmacy-level stock-outs continue to be reported in 2026. Cost remains $1,600–$2,200/month cash price; copay card available for commercially insured patients.

  • Source: Medfinder — Xifaxan Shortage 2026 ↗

2026 Salix GI Health Scholars Program

Salix announced the 2026 Gastrointestinal Health Scholars Program; application period closed May 6, 2026. Routine disease-area investment; no material brand strategy signal.

Competitive Intelligence▼

HE Space — New ACG Guidelines (March 2026)

The American College of Gastroenterology published updated HE clinical guidelines in March 2026. Key for Xifaxan: rifaximin remains recommended as add-on to lactulose for patients with recurrent overt HE. Guideline also endorses zinc supplementation for patients failing lactulose + rifaximin. New emphasis on covert/minimal HE detection and nutrition (1.2–1.5 g/kg/d protein).

  • Source: ACG Clinical Guideline — Hepatic Encephalopathy ↗ | Guideline Central ↗

HE Pipeline — FMT (MASTERPIECE Trial)

The MASTERPIECE trial is investigating whether microbiota transplant therapy (FMT) can prevent future HE episodes in veterans with cirrhosis. Still in trial phase; not an immediate competitive threat but worth tracking as long-horizon alternative to rifaximin in the HE maintenance space.

  • Source: Guideline Central HE Trial Preview ↗

IBS-D — Ibsrela (Tenapanor) Surging, But in IBS-C

Ibsrela had 73% revenue growth in 2025 ($274M) and management guided 2026 to $410–430M. Ardelyx also dosed first patient in ACCEL Phase 3 (Jan 2026), expanding into chronic idiopathic constipation (CIC). Important note: Ibsrela is approved for IBS-C, not IBS-D — not a direct Xifaxan competitor, but competes for GI prescriber mindshare and market expansion in the bowel disorder space. A new patent was issued covering tenapanor formulations through 2042.

  • Source: Ardelyx FY2025 Results ↗ | GlobeNewswire ↗

IBS-D — Viberzi (Eluxadoline)

Viberzi continues to be limited by sphincter of Oddi spasm risk and prescribing restrictions. No new notable developments found in the last 48–72 hours.

IBS-C — Linzess (Linaclotide)

Linzess generated ~$864.5M in US net sales in 2025. Dominates IBS-C. Generic linaclotide now more widely available. Not a direct Xifaxan IBS-D competitor.

Bausch Health sued Amneal Pharmaceuticals after Amneal filed an ANDA for generic rifaximin 550 mg (IBS-D indication), asserting patents are invalid/unenforceable/not infringed. Strategic Signals▼
  • RED-C failure is the headline risk. The SSD reformulation was Bausch's primary mechanism for extending Xifaxan's commercial life post-exclusivity. With that gone, the cliff in 2027–2029 is steeper and less manageable. Analysts are now calling for "Plan B" with no clear answer yet.
  • ACG guidelines reinforce current positioning. The March 2026 update locked rifaximin into standard-of-care for HE alongside lactulose. This is a durable tailwind for the base brand — but doesn't solve the generic threat.
  • Generic entry window: 2027 at the earliest (550mg). The 30-month Hatch-Waxman stays give Salix at least 18–24 months of additional legal cover. Amneal is the lead filer to watch.
  • Q1 2026 performance signals brand health is intact short-term. 21% Xifaxan revenue growth and $639M Salix quarter are strong. The commercial machine is functioning.
  • Ibsrela's growth trajectory bears watching. Not a direct competitor, but Ardelyx is building a dominant GI franchise with a 2042 patent horizon. If Ibsrela expands into further GI indications, it becomes a prescriber attention competitor for Xifaxan.
  • FMT pipeline is a longer-range watch item for HE. MASTERPIECE and other microbiome trials are still years from market, but they signal where academic thought leadership is heading on HE prevention.
