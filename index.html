Xifaxan Updates▼
  • No new last-48–72h developments found. Searches across Xifaxan, rifaximin, Salix/Bausch, and HE/IBS-D returned no fresh items dated this week. The most recent material event remains the RED-C readout (Jan 23, 2026).
  • RED-C Phase 3 failure (Jan 23, 2026) — still the defining event. Both pivotal trials (RED-C-3131 and RED-C-3132, >1,000 patients across 398 sites in 17 countries) of amorphous-rifaximin solid soluble dispersion (SSD) for primary prevention of HE missed their primary endpoint — SSD failed to significantly delay first onset of HE vs. placebo. Drug was safe/well-tolerated. Bausch shares fell ~12.4% on the news. SSD had been modeled as a potential ~$2B/yr product and the intended growth bridge past 2028 exclusivity. Source: Bausch IR ↗, Fierce Pharma ↗, AInvest ↗.
  • Franchise weight: XIFAXAN was ~20% of Bausch FY2024 revenue; with SSD out, there is no internal next-gen successor to defend the post-2028 cliff. Source: AInvest ↗.
  • Access friction persists. Not on the FDA shortage list, but single-source manufacturing, high cash price (~$19,200–$26,400/yr for HE maintenance), and insurance barriers continue to make rifaximin practically hard to fill. Source: Medfinder ↗, Medical News Today ↗.
Competitive Intelligence▼
  • HE landscape — guideline tailwind for rifaximin. The new ACG HE guideline (Mar 3, 2026) keeps lactulose + rifaximin as the maintenance backbone, recommending adding rifaximin for recurrent OHE on lactulose, then zinc, then shunt embolization / BCAA support for refractory cases. This reinforces Xifaxan's entrenched add-on position in HE even without SSD. Source: Guideline Central ↗.
  • IBS-D — Ibsrela is the momentum story. Ibsrela (tenapanor) posted $274.2M in 2025 sales, up 73% YoY. No generic; patents protect to ~2033. This is the clearest share-taker in the adjacent IBS franchise. Source: Xtalks ↗, GoodRx ↗.
  • Viberzi (eluxadoline) remains constrained by safety/prescribing restrictions — limited growth. Linzess/linaclotide now faces generic linaclotide. Source: GoodRx ↗, SingleCare ↗.
  • Pipeline to watch: EnteroBiotix's EBX-102-02 microbiome therapeutic showed clinically meaningful IBS-SSS improvement vs. placebo in its TrIuMPH IBS-D trial; gut-brain (delta-opioid agonist) approaches advancing. Early but directionally relevant to long-term IBS-D share. Source: DDW ↗, Drug Topics ↗.
  • Market frame: Global IBS treatment market ~$3.64B (2024) → projected ~$6.02B by 2030 (8.8% CAGR). Source: Xtalks ↗.
Patent & Legal Watch▼
  • Generic entry timeline holds at ~2028–2029. Settlements allow Teva, Sun, and Sandoz generics starting 2028; modeled launch estimates cluster around Oct 2, 2029. Source: Fierce Pharma ↗, DrugPatentWatch ↗.
  • Amneal — tentative ANDA approval (rifaximin 550 mg, IBS-D). Tentative only; blocked by litigation and the 30-month stay from the 2024 Salix/Bausch suit. Cannot launch until patent/stay clears. Source: HCPLive ↗, Amneal IR ↗.
  • Norwich/Alvogen — blocked. Supreme Court declined to hear Norwich's appeal; D.C. District Court (Apr 2025) denied Norwich's preliminary injunction on its 550 mg ANDA. HE-use patents held. Source: Conexiant ↗, Law360 ↗.
  • Antitrust overhang: Bausch and Teva face "pay-for-delay" scheme claims tied to the Xifaxan settlement — worth monitoring as a wildcard on the 2028 timeline. Source: Bloomberg Law ↗.
Strategic Signals▼
  • The successor gap is now the central risk. RED-C's failure removed Bausch's organic defense for the post-2028 cliff. Expect pressure toward BD/licensing, label/lifecycle moves on existing Xifaxan, or cost action — not a clinical fix.
  • Guideline = near-term moat. ACG reaffirmation keeps Xifaxan structurally embedded in HE maintenance through patent life; demand is sticky regardless of pipeline setbacks.
  • Generic clock is the real countdown: ~2028 authorized entry, ~2029 broad. The antitrust suit is the only plausible accelerant — watch it.
  • Adjacent share leakage: Ibsrela's 73% growth is the competitive signal to track in IBS-D, not Viberzi/Linzess.
  • Implication for BVL (if servicing this account): messaging gravity shifts from "innovation/next-gen" to "established standard of care + access support." Pre-loss-of-exclusivity planning (DTC, patient assistance, payer) is the logical workstream.
