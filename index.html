Use it to pressure-test whether your content brief will survive AI search — not just Google.

Xifaxan Updates▼

Q1 2026 Revenue Strong — Despite Pipeline Setback

Xifaxan was the primary growth driver for Salix in Q1 2026, posting 21% revenue growth year-over-year. Salix segment revenues reached $639M for the quarter (up 18% vs. Q1 2025 at $542M). Bausch Health reaffirmed full-year 2026 revenue guidance as of April 29, 2026. Source: Bausch Health Q1 2026 Results – SEC 8-K ↗

RED-C Phase III Program Failure (January 23, 2026)

Both RED-C trials — evaluating amorphous rifaximin solid soluble dispersion (SSD) for primary prevention of hepatic encephalopathy in cirrhosis patients with no prior HE — failed to meet their primary endpoint. The global program enrolled 1,000+ patients across 398 sites in 17 countries. The drug was safe and well-tolerated but showed no statistically significant delay in first HE onset vs. placebo. CEO Tom Appio: "We are currently reviewing the full dataset to determine potential new development opportunities." This closes the primary prevention HE indication. Sources: Clinical Trials Arena ↗ | Bausch Health PR Newswire ↗

Supply: 200mg Tablets on Allocation

No formal FDA shortage listing for Xifaxan, but 200mg tablets are on allocation as of May 2026. Practical access issues persist — driven by high cost ($1,600–$2,200/30-day supply for 550mg) and insurance barriers, not manufacturing disruption. Sources: Medfinder Shortage Update ↗ | ASHP Shortage Detail ↗

Competitive Intelligence▼

HE Landscape — Rifaximin Still Standard, But FMT Emerging

The 2026 ACG Clinical Guideline for Hepatic Encephalopathy (published March 2026) reinforces rifaximin as the recommended add-on for patients with overt HE experiencing recurrence on lactulose. No new entrants have displaced lactulose + rifaximin as the first-line combo. However, the MASTERPIECE trial is evaluating fecal microbiota transplantation (FMT) capsules from healthy donors to prevent recurrent HE in veterans with cirrhosis — a potential long-term competitive signal. Sources: ACG Guideline 2026 – LWW ↗ | Guideline Central Summary ↗

IBS-D: Ibsrela Growing Fast

Ibsrela (tenapanor, Ardelyx) posted $274M in 2025 revenue — a 73% YoY increase — solidifying its position as a growing IBS-C/IBS-D adjacent competitor. Linzess (linaclotide, AbbVie) remains dominant at ~$864M US net sales in 2025. Neither has a generic. Viberzi (eluxadoline) usage remains constrained by sphincter of Oddi spasm safety risk. Source: Xtalks IBS Market 2026 ↗

IBS-D Pipeline Signals

  • EnteroBiotix EBX-102-02: Positive Phase IIa results from TrIuMPH trial — a full-spectrum microbiome therapeutic showing clinically meaningful improvements in IBS symptom severity vs. placebo. Phase IIb planned. Source: DDW Online ↗
  • CIN-103 (CinPhloro Pharma): Phase 2 trial underway for IBS-D — non-opioid small molecule targeting motility, secretion, and visceral pain.

Patent & Legal Watch▼

Amneal ANDA Litigation — 30-Month Stay Active

Bausch Health/Salix filed suit against Amneal Pharmaceuticals in April 2024 after Amneal filed an ANDA for generic rifaximin 550mg. The Hatch-Waxman 30-month stay is active — earliest FDA approval of Amneal's generic: approximately October 2026, assuming no court ruling. Amneal asserts relevant patents are invalid/unenforceable/not infringed. No resolution reported as of this brief. Sources: Drug Store News ↗ | The Pharmaletter ↗

Federal Circuit Precedent Favors Salix

The Federal Circuit previously affirmed patent victory for Salix vs. Norwich Pharmaceuticals, upholding the rifaximin Orange Book patents and restricting ANDA approval. This sets favorable precedent for the Amneal case. Source: PatSnap Eureka ↗

Patent Cliff Projected 2028–2029

Current exclusivity is projected to hold through 2028–2029. Multiple ANDA filers (Amneal, Padagis, Sandoz — tentative approval) remain staged for launch at expiration. Source: Drug Patent Watch ↗

Its absence forces Bausch/Salix to defend the existing HE recurrence and IBS-D base rather than expand the indication. Watch for any new development pathway announcements from the dataset review.

5. Bausch Health parent under pressure. Q1 2026 included a $1.43B goodwill impairment charge, despite strong Salix performance. Bausch's financial structure could eventually affect Salix operational priorities or a potential separation/sale. This is a background risk for the brand.
