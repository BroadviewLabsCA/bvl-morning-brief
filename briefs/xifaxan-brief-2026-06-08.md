**RED-C Phase III Program Failure (January 23, 2026)**
Both RED-C trials — evaluating amorphous rifaximin solid soluble dispersion (SSD) for *primary prevention* of hepatic encephalopathy in cirrhosis patients with no prior HE — failed to meet their primary endpoint. The global program enrolled 1,000+ patients across 398 sites in 17 countries. The drug was safe and well-tolerated but showed no statistically significant delay in first HE onset vs. placebo. CEO Tom Appio: *"We are currently reviewing the full dataset to determine potential new development opportunities."* This closes the primary prevention HE indication. Sources: [Clinical Trials Arena](https://www.clinicaltrialsarena.com/news/bausch-health-rifaximin-ssd-hepatic-encephalopathy-phase-iii-miss/) | [Bausch Health PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/bausch-health-provides-update-on-red-c-phase-3-clinical-trials-302668924.html)

## Competitive Intelligence

**HE Landscape — Rifaximin Still Standard, But FMT Emerging**
The 2026 ACG Clinical Guideline for Hepatic Encephalopathy (published March 2026) reinforces rifaximin as the recommended add-on for patients with overt HE experiencing recurrence on lactulose. No new entrants have displaced lactulose + rifaximin as the first-line combo. However, the MASTERPIECE trial is evaluating fecal microbiota transplantation (FMT) capsules from healthy donors to prevent recurrent HE in veterans with cirrhosis — a potential long-term competitive signal. Sources: [ACG Guideline 2026 – LWW](https://journals.lww.com/ajg/fulltext/2026/03000/acg_clinical_guideline__hepatic_encephalopathy.12.aspx) | [Guideline Central Summary](https://www.guidelinecentral.com/insights/mar-2026-acg-hepaticencephalopathy-guideline-spotlight/)

**IBS-D: Ibsrela Growing Fast**

Source: [Xtalks IBS Market 2026](https://xtalks.com/ibs-awareness-month-2026-the-hidden-realities-of-ibs-and-the-ibs-treatment-market-4725/)

## Strategic Signals

1. **RED-C failure removes the primary HE prevention upside.** This was the key pipeline catalyst for Xifaxan's next growth chapter. Its absence forces Bausch/Salix to defend the existing HE recurrence and IBS-D base rather than expand the indication. Watch for any new development pathway announcements from the dataset review.

2. **Xifaxan's core business is healthy.** +21% revenue growth in Q1 despite no new indications is a strong signal — the recurrence/IBS-D franchise has pricing power and sticky prescribing. The brand is not in decline.

3. **Amneal 30-month stay clock is ticking.** With the stay expiring around October 2026, the next several months of litigation activity will be critical. A favorable ruling before expiration extends protection; a miss or settlement could accelerate generic entry.

4. **Microbiome therapies are the long-cycle competitive threat.** FMT (HE) and EBX-102-02 (IBS-D) are years from market, but both target Xifaxan's core mechanisms of action (gut dysbiosis modulation). Worth monitoring trial progression.

5. **Bausch Health parent under pressure.** Q1 2026 included a $1.43B goodwill impairment charge, despite strong Salix performance. Bausch's financial structure could eventually affect Salix operational priorities or a potential separation/sale. This is a background risk for the brand.
