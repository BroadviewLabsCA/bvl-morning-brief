## Strategic Signals

**RED-C Failure Impact: Significant but Not Catastrophic**
Rifaximin SSD (amorphous formulation for primary HE prevention) missed primary endpoints in both RED-C-3131 and RED-C-3132 trials - enrolling 1,000+ patients across 17 countries. Stock dropped ~10% on the announcement (January 2026). Bausch is reviewing the full dataset for alternative development paths. Analysts had pegged this as a potential $2B+ asset.
- This removes a key Xifaxan lifecycle extension strategy in HE.
- Core Xifaxan (existing indications) is unaffected - and growing.
- Bausch has pivoted pipeline attention to larsucosterol for alcohol-associated hepatitis.

**New ACG Guideline is a Tailwind**
The March 2026 ACG HE guideline strengthening rifaximin's role as standard of care reinforces prescriber behavior ahead of generic entry. Salix likely to use this in promotional materials and HCP outreach.

**Ibsrela's 73% Growth is the Key Watch Metric**
In IBS-D, Ibsrela is the most aggressive competitive threat by momentum. With patent protection to 2033 and rapid adoption, it represents a structural long-term share shift away from Xifaxan for IBS-D patients.
