## Strategic Signals

**Franchise at an Inflection Point**
The RED-C failure removes Bausch's most anticipated Xifaxan lifecycle extension. With the patent cliff locked in around 2028–2029 and no successor formulation now in pivotal trials, the Xifaxan franchise is entering a 3-year revenue defense window. Expect Bausch to prioritize:
- Aggressive litigation to push generics past 2028
- Pricing and access optimization to maximize near-term revenue
- Potential new investigator-sponsored or label expansion efforts

**ACG Guidelines = Near-Term Commercial Tailwind**
The March 2026 ACG guideline update explicitly recommending rifaximin add-on to lactulose in recurrent OHE gives Salix a clear promotional hook in the HE segment. Expect this to be activated in sales and medical education efforts.

**IBS-D Microbiome Science Shifting**
The convergence of microbiome-based data (EnteroBiotix, FODMAP/rifaximin comparison study) signals that IBS-D treatment narratives are shifting toward personalized, microbiome-informed approaches. Xifaxan's mechanism fits this story, but Salix will need to proactively engage on this science to maintain positioning.

**No Immediate Threat, But Watch Amneal**
The most likely near-term disruption is Amneal resolving or settling litigation and launching an authorized generic earlier than 2028. A settlement in the next 12–18 months is possible given the Teva/Sun/Sandoz precedents.
