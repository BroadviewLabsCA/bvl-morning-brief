**RED-C Phase III Failure (January 23, 2026).** Bausch Health's rifaximin SSD (amorphous solid soluble dispersion), a next-generation reformulation positioned as Xifaxan's successor in HE primary prevention, failed both Phase III trials. The RED-C program enrolled 1,000+ patients across 398 sites in 17 countries — neither trial met the primary endpoint of delaying first HE episode in cirrhosis patients with no prior HE history. Bausch recorded a $1.426B goodwill impairment in Q1 2026 as a result. The company is reviewing the full dataset for potential new development paths but has no clear Plan B for HE expansion. Sources: [Clinical Trials Arena](https://www.clinicaltrialsarena.com/news/bausch-health-rifaximin-ssd-hepatic-encephalopathy-phase-iii-miss/) · [FiercePharma](https://www.fiercepharma.com/pharma/bausch-healths-2b-pursuit-xifaxan-successor-hits-wall-phase-3-failure) · [Citeline/Scrip](https://insights.citeline.com/scrip/r-and-d/clinical-trials/bausch-health-needs-plan-b-after-red-c-trials-fail-C5RTDGEGG5DGDLIQINRUVUU3PE/)

## Competitive Intelligence

**HE Standard of Care Reinforced.** The American College of Gastroenterology published updated HE guidelines in March 2026. Key recommendation: for patients with overt HE on lactulose experiencing recurrent episodes, rifaximin is recommended as add-on therapy. Zinc supplementation is added for persistent symptoms despite lactulose + rifaximin. The guidelines maintain Xifaxan's entrenched position in the recurrent HE maintenance protocol. Source: [Guideline Central – March 2026 ACG HE Guideline](https://www.guidelinecentral.com/insights/mar-2026-acg-hepaticencephalopathy-guideline-spotlight/)

**HE Pipeline Threat Assessment.** Several therapies are in active development that could eventually compete with or complement rifaximin in HE:
- **VE303** (Vedanta Biosciences) — microbiome-based; Phase 2 for HE treatment (~100 patients in trials)
- **GR3027** (Umecrine Cognition) — GABA-A neurosteroid antagonist; anticipated US filing ~2027
- **EBX-102** (EnteroBiotix) — microbiome-based
- **YAQ007** (Yaqrit Discovery) — mechanism targets hyperammonemia
- **25% IV Albumin** (Grifols) — adjunctive HE management
- **MASTERPIECE trial** — microbiota transplant capsules vs. HE recurrence in veterans with cirrhosis, ongoing

Source: [DelveInsight HE Market Report, May 2026](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/05/04/3287025/0/en/Hepatic-Encephalopathy-Market-Set-for-Growth-at-a-1.2-CAGR-Through-2036-Driven-by-Emerging-Therapies-and-Rising-Disease-Burden-DelveInsight.html) · [Guideline Central – May 2026 HE Trial Preview](https://www.guidelinecentral.com/insights/may-2026-hepatic-encephalopathy-trial-preview/)

## Strategic Signals

**Xifaxan revenue is growing despite pipeline failure.** The 21% Q1 2026 revenue growth is notable — commercial execution at Salix is strong even as the long-term succession plan collapsed. This makes defending the patent wall through 2029 the single most important near-term priority for Bausch/Salix.

**RED-C failure creates a strategic gap in primary HE prevention.** No approved therapy exists for primary prevention in cirrhosis patients without prior HE episodes. Pipeline entrants (GR3027, VE303) are targeting exactly this unmet need. Xifaxan is not positioned here — its label covers recurrent HE, not primary prevention.

**ACG guidelines are a tailwind.** The March 2026 ACG guideline update explicitly recommends rifaximin add-on for recurrent HE on lactulose. This reinforces prescriber behavior and creates headroom for continued formulary positioning.

**HE market growth modest but durable.** DelveInsight projects the HE market at 1.2% CAGR through 2036 — not explosive, but stable. Rifaximin's position as the established standard of care makes it hard to displace absent a major clinical disruption.

**IBS-D: no immediate competitive disruption.** The IBS-D pipeline remains sparse. High-growth IBS competitors (Ibsrela, Linzess) are IBS-C focused and don't directly threaten Xifaxan's IBS-D positioning. The real threat is generic entry ~2029, not brand competition.
