## Policy & Risk

**California AB 489 (effective Jan 1, 2026)** prohibits AI systems from using language or design that implies a healthcare license or that AI is a human clinician. Applies to developers and deployers. If any BVL deliverable includes AI-generated content that could be read as coming from a licensed professional, this is a compliance flag.

**State legislatures are moving faster than federal.** California, Colorado, and others are rolling out laws targeting AI that impersonates doctors, "algorithmic discrimination," and clinical oversight requirements. No federal comprehensive AI law yet — patchwork is the operative word for 2026.

**Key risk for BVL:** Clients will increasingly ask "is this AI-generated?" Transparency requirements are hardening. BVL's AI use policy and disclosure posture should be client-ready now, not reactive.

**Mental health AI and minor-facing AI** are drawing the most state legislative attention. If any BVL client is in behavioral health or pediatrics, prioritize reviewing their AI-adjacent content strategy.
